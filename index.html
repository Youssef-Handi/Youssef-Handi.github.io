About

I am a Ph.D. researcher in Mathematics and Statistics at Université du Québec à Montréal (UQAM), where I develop statistical methods for modeling complex dependence structures. My work focuses on copulas and copula-based regression frameworks for multivariate binary outcomes, with extensions to mixed continuous–discrete data. I am particularly interested in robust and asymmetric regression, as well as inference procedures for quantiles and expectiles in discrete regression settings. More broadly, my interests include Bayesian statistics, spatio-temporal modeling, and statistical learning.

Before my Ph.D., I completed an M.Sc. in Computer Science and Applied Mathematics and worked as a research assistant at Hydro-Québec’s research institute (IREQ), designing Bayesian frameworks that combine expert opinions with performance data and implementing MCMC-based prediction tools for reliability. I also have applied experience in the financial sector as a data scientist, developing machine-learning models for credit risk classification, stress-testing models, and data pipelines with quality controls for analytical platforms.
